How the probability is calculated
The chance of finishing above the target is the normal CDF of ln(price / target) ÷ σ, where σ is the standard deviation of the move over the time left. σ comes from the realized volatility of recent consensus prices (variance rate per second × √horizon), so it adapts to live conditions instead of assuming a fixed tick rate. Because the contract settles on a 60-second BRTI average, the variance horizon is shrunk to T − ⅔·window (the variance of a time-average, not a single endpoint). Drift is ignored — over minutes it's negligible next to volatility.

Limits worth keeping in mind: the volatility estimate is noisy, a quiet tape can understate real risk, and a random-walk model can't anticipate jumps or news. Treat the percentage as a rough gauge, not a price. Manual decision aid only — not financial advice.
